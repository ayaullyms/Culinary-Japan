Ramen Recipe

Ramen is a popular Japanese noodle soup dish that has gained international acclaim. It typically consists of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a meat or fish-based broth, flavored with soy sauce or miso. The dish is often topped with ingredients such as sliced pork, dried seaweed, green onions, and a boiled egg.

There are many regional variations of ramen across Japan, each with its own unique flavor profile and ingredients. For example, Tonkotsu ramen features a rich, creamy pork bone broth, while Shoyu ramen is known for its savory soy sauce-based broth.

Ramen is not just a meal but a cultural experience, reflecting the diverse culinary traditions and preferences of different regions in Japan.

Here’s a basic recipe to get you started.

Ingredients:

  • 200g ramen noodles
  • 1 liter chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon miso paste
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 egg, soft-boiled (optional)
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup bok choy or spinach
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat a bit of oil and sauté the garlic and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Add the broth, soy sauce, and miso paste. Bring to a simmer.
  3. Add the mushrooms and cook for about 5 minutes.
  4. Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions and set aside.
  5. Add the bok choy or spinach to the broth and cook for 2-3 minutes until wilted.
  6. Divide the cooked noodles into bowls. Ladle the hot broth and vegetables over the noodles.
  7. If using, top with a soft-boiled egg and sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade ramen!
